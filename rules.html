How to Win:

To win a Bitwise game, a player must complete the 4Bit sequences, indicated by one of their Black 4Bit Cards, to score a Bit Point and turn the corresponding White 4Bit Card face down. A player wins based on the following criteria:

2 Players Score 4 Bit Points
3 Players Score 3 Bit Points
4 Players Score 2 Bit Points

Set Up:

To set up the game, follow these steps:

  1. Every player takes a Tutorial Card face up in front of them.
  2. Shuffle the White 4Bit Cards deck and place it face down on the upper left corner of the 5x5 Bit Grid.
  3. Draw 8 cards and place them face up to complete the first row and the first column of the grid.
  4. Find the corresponding 4Bit Cards from the Black 4Bit Deck and form a face-down pile with them (the Black 4Bit Deck will not be used again in the setup).
  5. Shuffle the face-down pile and deal to each player:
    6. 2 Players 4 Black 4Bit Cards
    3 Players 3 Black 4Bit Cards
    4 Players 4 Black 4Bit Cards
  6. Then, shuffle Bit cards, Gate cards, and Special Cards to form the Deck.
  7. Deal 8 cards to each player clockwise from the Deck.
  8. The player who looked at any kind of screen most recently starts first.

In a Turn:

During their turn, a player should:

  1. Draw a card from the Deck.
  2. Play up to 1 card of each Type (Bit, Special, Gate) in any order unless a played card states otherwise.
  3. If a player does not play any cards, they must draw another card from the Deck.
  4. Afterward, pass their turn to the next player on the left (clockwise).
