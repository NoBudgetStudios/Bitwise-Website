How to win:

A player who completes the 4Bit sequences, indicated by one of their Black 4Bit Cards, scores a Bit Point, and turns the corresponding White 4Bit Card face down.
A player wins by scoring:
2 Players, 4 Bit Points
3 Players, 3 Bit Points
4 Player, 2 Bit Points

Set Up:

Every player takes a Tutorial Card face up in front of them. Shuffle the White 4Bit Cards deck and place it face down on the upper left corner of the 5x5 Bit Grid. Draw 8 cards and place them face up completing the first row and the first column of the grid. Then find the corresponding 4Bit Cards from the Black 4Bit Deck and form a face down pile with them (Black 4Bit Deck will not be used again in the setup). Then shuffle the face down pile and deal to each Player:
2 Players, 4 Black 4Bit Cards
3 Players, 3 Black 4Bit Cards
4 Player, 2 Black 4Bit Cards
Then, shuffle Bit cards, Gate cards and Special Cards forming the Deck. Deal 8 cards to each player clockwise from the Deck. The player who looked at any kind of screen most recently, starts first.

In a Turn:

A player, in their turn, first draws a card from the Bit Deck. Then they can play up to 1 card of each Type (Bit, Special, Gate) in any order, unless a played card states otherwise. If a player does not play any card, they draw a card from the Special Deck. Eitherway, right after that, they pass their turn to the next player on the left (clockwise).
